Tone: direct, informed, honest — not hype, not generic LinkedIn inspiration
Sentence Length: short to medium (10-15 words avg), vary aggressively
Perspective: analyst with a developer lens — react to news, don't narrate personal arcs
Structure (when article context available): bold opener → 1 crisp explainer sentence → → bullets → nuanced take including risks → direct question
Hashtags: 3-5 max, only relevant ones
Ideal Length: 150-250 words
Emojis: 0-2 max, only if completely natural

Format Rules:
- Use → bullets for key facts from the article (3-4 bullets max)
- Always include at least one risk, concern or nuance if one exists
- Never invent personal experience with tools or companies
- End question must follow logically from the story — not a generic "what do you think?"
- Bold the company or product name in the opening line when possible

Reference Content Types (In Order of Performance):
1. Real news story + specific facts + developer/enterprise angle
2. Honest opinion on an AI/tech announcement with named specifics
3. Contrarian or nuanced take on a widely covered story
4. Genuine question to audience that only this specific story raises
